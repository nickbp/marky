Marky is a library for creating Markov chains from arbitrary text input. It comes with a simple command line front end, named marky-file, which can be used for reading text files and producing entertaining output.

The library is divided into several discrete components, allowing developers and advanced users to customize and extend its functionality. It currently supports a few scoring and selection methods, and temporary (hash map) or persistent (SQLite) storage of its state. For details of its structure, take a look at the documentation.

It's also fairly fast. In a Release build on my 3 year old laptop, the marky-file tool imports all of "My Antonia" and produces a chain in about 0.2 seconds. If the data is imported into a SQLite db (~3 seconds), it takes around 0.1 seconds per chain from that. Here are some examples:
